Book Cover (AR)

Representing themes of literature through visually striking book cover redesign. AR component elevates the message through hidden layers of visual design.

Role

Illustration
3D Modelling
Visual Design

Team

Chan Ik Chris Jung

Timeline

September - November, 2021

Explore

AR

Challenge

The first impression is important when people judge the book by its cover. Great cover design can elevate the value of the literary works and inform readers what can be expected from the content.

I was always interested in AR project but lack experience. The question is how do I create compelling book cover design with AR component that elevates the message?

Candidates

Three books were considered to explore the translation of thematic content to visual design. "The Day of the Triffids" deals with human greed and self destruction. "Sidetracked" is about people trapped in inescapable maze of greed and hate.

"The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" is about identity and duality of people. After choosing books in different genre, I’ve made quick conceptual drawings to determine the important theme and rhetorical tropes.

Choice

After another pass at refining each design, I’ve chose the most compelling book to proceed the project with. I loved how “The Day of The Triffids” told the story of our own blindness to greed is responsible for the fall of mankind.

The back cover is heavily inspired by the hunting pictures from human shadow etched in stone after nuclear bomb fell on Hiroshima.

People as greedy monster

Meteoroid as eyes blind by greed

Nuclear shadow as destructive human nature

Addtional Layer

The AR component design came together easier than the book cover design. Three designs, depicting attempt at escaping, inescapable nature of human greed, and self destruction were considered.

The human greed was the best choice because it provides more cohesive narrative layer to the book cover design. It depicts inescapable nature of human greed as a maze and a meteoroid that acts as a search light trying to find its next victim.

Reflections

This project certainly pushed boundaries of my design and technical skills. There were a lot of interesting topics in the book, but I’ve learned to find a single theme that could act as an anchor point to tell visually engaging story.

Combining narrative into the visual design is something that I enjoyed the most about the project. I’ll continuously develop the skill and hope to work on similar project in the future.
